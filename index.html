About


I am a machine learning researcher at Freie Universität Berlin supported by MATH+. Currently, my interest lies in the combination of ideas from high-dimensional probability, nonparametric statistics and stochastic processes for inference in inverse problems, time series and sequential data.

I am also with dida in the role of a machine learning scientist, where I work on the development and deployment of large-scale deep learning systems for NLP and computer vision with applications in information extraction and remote sensing.

In 2022, I obtained my PhD under the supervision of Christof Schütte in the Biocomputing group at Freie Universität Berlin. In my dissertation, I aimed to contribute to theory of nonparametric dimensionality reduction of Markov processes (geared towards applications in molecular dynamics and fluid mechanics). During my PhD program, I spent some time in aerospace engineering at Rolls-Royce Deutschland, where I worked on machine learning applications for controller software and anomaly detection in aircraft engines.

My current work together with Claudia Schillings addresses problems in uncertainty quantification and the data-driven numerical analysis of stochastic partial differential equations.
